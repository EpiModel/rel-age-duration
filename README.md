# Relational Duration and the Age of Extant Relationships

This Shiny app demonstrates the connections between relational duration (how long a relationship lasts) and relational age (how old an ongoing relationship is when observed). It simulates a user-specified number of relationships with configurable mean durations and distribution types (geometric, uniform, or fixed), then calculates metrics for relationships that exist on a randomly chosen observation day.

The app illustrates the counter-intuitive property of memoryless distributions: for geometrically distributed durations, the expected age of an extant relationship equals the overall mean duration, rather than being shorter as one might expect.

Available at: https://epimodel.github.io/rel-age-duration
